Project 4: Information in Motion

Graphic design is frequently used to interpret, to persuade, or to sell things—products, services, ideas—but it also has the capacity to spread knowledge and bring clarity to complex topics. Information design is the practice of visually presenting information so that it can be understood thoroughly and efficiently, and through this sort of practice designers can embrace the potential civic value of their work. Information design can be, at its best, a public service. Designers have a unique ability to clarify and share important information with an audience to whom this knowledge might be essential.

21 Mar 2017

Project 3: The Reaction Gif

Animated GIFs, a file format introduced in 1987, are not new technology. Still, they've been given new life in contemporary internet culture. They are portable (due to their small file size and wide support across many platforms), accessible (easy for most internet users to find, digest, and even make themselves), and their quick, looping, bite-size format lends itself well to humor and wit. All of this makes the animated GIF a popular form of online visual communication.

Project 2: Kinetic Type

Typography has the potential to (literally) take on another dimension when used in motion graphics. As with our previous work, this project will explore the process of combining traditional graphic design elements (such as composition, color, shape, depth, contrast, etc.) with a specific focus on typography and the dynamic, unique communicative potential of type in motion.
