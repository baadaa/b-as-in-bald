Project 4: Information in Motion

Graphic design is frequently used to interpret, to persuade, or to sell things—products, services, ideas—but it also has the capacity to spread knowledge and bring clarity to complex topics. Information design is the practice of visually presenting information so that it can be understood thoroughly and efficiently, and through this sort of practice designers can embrace the potential civic value of their work. Information design can be, at its best, a public service. Designers have a unique ability to clarify and share important information with an audience to whom this knowledge might be essential.

Image: Oil'd by Chris Harmon

This project focuses on information design in motion. Much like how motion design is well suited to convey a particularly complex or multifaceted narrative, it also has the potential to communicate complex information or data with a lot of nuance and clarity. A timely and pressing topic that lends itself to time-based information design is the science surrounding global climate change. From the Environmental Protection Agency, on climate change:

“Our Earth is warming. Earth’s average temperature has risen by 1.5°F over the past century, and is projected to rise another 0.5 to 8.6°F over the next hundred years. Small changes in the average temperature of the planet can translate to large and potentially dangerous shifts in climate and weather.”

Your assignment is to create an information-driven motion graphic that utilizes space, time, motion, and sound to communicate a message about climate change. This motion graphic can incorporate data, typography, imagery, icons, charts / graphs, etc., and should include an audio component of your choosing. You should use a minimum of 2-3 of the climate change facts provided to construct a thoughtful narrative / progression (feel free to rewrite or rephrase these facts as needed to achieve whatever tone is appropriate for your chosen audience). Define a particular audience for this motion graphic — who would benefit from knowing more about this information? — and consider this audience when making decisions about how to communicate the information.

There are two different but related possible approaches to time-based information design:

  1. Animated Data Visualization: this approach focuses on communicating complex data and the visual communication of information. As with all time-based media there is a narrative, but this narrative is more of a simple progression than a developed story.
  2. Infographic-Based Narrative in Motion: this approach does communicate facts, data, and information, but this information serves to develop a more complex narrative and tell a story.

As is often the case with different ways to practice design, it is very possible for there to be some overlap between these two approaches.

Objectives

  • Create an information-driven motion graphic that clearly and compellingly communicates a message
  • Define and consider a specific audience for your motion graphic
  • Thoughtfully integrate typography, imagery (representational form and/or abstract form), audio, and motion
  • Continue to investigate the combination of traditional design attributes (color, composition, shape, depth, tension, contrast) and time-based attributes (progression, pacing, rhythm, sequence, velocity, kinetic behavior)
  • Continue to investigate the development of a narrative arc
  • Continue to investigate typography in motion: traditional typographic characteristics (typeface, type size, kerning, tracking, leading, etc.) in addition to kinetic characteristics (motion typography and fluid typography)
  • Continue to develop an advanced and complex relationship between audio and moving visual elements
  • Show a high level of technical proficiency and skill in using After Effects

Project deliverables

  • Final time-based motion graphic
    • Composition size: 1280 x 720
    • Duration: approximately 20 seconds long
  • Drawn storyboards (for 3 concepts)
  • 3-4 key frames (for selected concept)
  • Rough drafts (as uploaded to Vimeo for each in-progress critique)
