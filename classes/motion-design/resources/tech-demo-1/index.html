Dashboard Basics

Project Panel

Where all of the imported files you will use to build your motion graphics will live (like a library of images, footage, etc)

Importing Footage

  • File > import footage or double-click Projects panel to open import window
  • Can select/import more than one files at a time
  • Can import an entire folder of files

Organizing Footage

  • You can move around, organize (in folders), rename imported footage in the Project panel without changing the files outside of After Effects
  • Note: if you want to replace footage in the project with a different/updated files, you can Control + click on the file in the Project panel and select Replace footage

Creating a Composition

  • Composition > New composition or click icon at bottom of project panel
  • For the purposes of this class and most of the time:
    • Choose PRESET HDV/HDTV 720 29.97
    • Leave the frame rate set at 29.97 frames per second
  • You will determine the duration of your composition and the background color in this menu, and can always go back and change it later by Control + clicking on the Composition in the Project panel — or by Command + K while in the selected composition

Timeline

  • The timeline shows the passage of time from left to right
  • Drag footage from project onto timeline
  • Inside a composition, drag the footage to move it around on the timeline
  • You can copy & paste, delete, or split footage
  • The playhead’s location on your timeline indicates at what point in time you are currently looking/working
  • Scrubbing (moving the playhead back and forth across the timeline)
  • Zoom in & zoom out (at bottom of timeline window, determines how many frames are visible in your timeline)
  • Layer ordering: the layers on the top of your timeline will appear on top of all other elements in your composition. The layers at the bottom of the your timeline will appear behind all other elements in your composition.
  • Buttons and switches:
    • Eyeball – turns layer on and off
    • Sound icon – turn on / off audio (if applicable)
    • Lock – prevents layer from being moved or edited
    • Toggle switches and modes button at bottom of timeline panel

Previewing

  • Time controls in the Preview panel – play/stop, move forward or back by frame
  • Note
    • Audio button and shortcut option
    • If working in CS6 instead of CC, previewing options may differ slightly. If pressing space bar to preview does not play audio, try Composition > Preview > RAM preview (or something similar)

Work Area:

  • Adjust at top of timeline panel with your mouse
  • Determines what on your timeline is previewed and rendered
  • Control + click on the work area to select Trim comp to work area

Viewing / working composition window:

  • Set scale (as percentage or can fit to current window size)
    • Can also zoom in/out using Command+ and Command- as usual
  • Setting the playback resolution:
    • Doesn’t degrade the actual resolution/quality of your video
    • Lower playback resolution = faster previews
    • Can set to Auto and After Effects will determine the resolution

Animating with Keyframes

Transform Settings

  • Transform drop down in timeline can adjust
    • Position
    • Scale
    • Rotation
    • Opacity
    • Anchor point: the central point around which the layer rotates/moves. You can also use the pan behind tool at the top of the screen.

Adding/removing keyframes

  • Place the playhead at the point on the timeline where you would like the keyframe to go
  • Click the stopwatch icon to the left of the Transform setting(s) to add a keyframe
  • Once you have added an initial keyframe, you will see an add or remove keyframe option appear on the far left to the relevant setting’s row. Scrubbing to a different point on the timeline and clicking on this diamond will add additional keyframe(s).
  • Additional keyframes are also added automatically if—after adding an initial keyframe—you scrub to a different point on the timeline and adjust the relevant setting.
  • Adding multiple keyframes at different points along the timeline is what causes motion, etc.
  • To delete key frames, just select the keyframe and hit delete key

Animating keyframes

  • Use keyframes for position, scale, rotation under Transform settings to create motion
  • Fade In effect
    • Add a solid layer: Layer > New > Solid
    • Make sure to position it as the top layer for fade-in to work
    • Add opacity keyframe at 100% opacity
    • Move playhead further along timeframe, adjust opacity to 0% / add keyframe
  • Fade Out effect
    • Same as Fade-In, except doing reverse opacity from 0% to 100%
  • Note
    • Easy Ease is a keyframe option related to Disney’s 12 Principles of Animation, specifically slow in & slow out. Easy Ease keyframes will result in an object that eases in and out of movement, making it feel more natural. To apply Easy Ease, Control + click on the keyframe, select Keyframe Assistant > Easy Ease, Easy Ease Out, or Easy Ease In
    • Adding a motion blur to a moving object can also make its movement appear more real. Just select the motion blur switch (looks like a ball flying up into the air) for that layer. Make sure motion blur is enabled for the composition as a whole, or else switching it on for one layer won’t do anything.

Working with Shapes & Text

Drawing Shapes in After Effects

  • To add a shape layer: Layer > New > Shape
  • You can draw/add a shape in After Effects similarly to other Adobe programs
    • Pen tool
    • Shape tool
  • Tools for drawing and adjusting shapes are at the top of the screen
    • Set fill color and stroke
    • Pen tools

Animating shapes

  • For shape layers containing shapes drawn in After Effects, you will have an additional set of dropdown options (in addition to Transform dropdown)
    • You can still apply all of the Transform settings to this layer in all the ways we’ve covered
  • Under the Shape # dropdown you can independently adjust and animate:
    • The stoke (the outline)
    • The fill (interior color)
    • The path
  • Can adjust the path similarly to how you would in illustrator: add/remove anchor points (called vertices in After Effects), drag them around (use pen tool), adjust a curve
  • Adjust the path in this way + add keyframes in order to animate the path, make the shape change shape over time
  • Try applying keyframes to stroke width
  • Try applying keyframes to fill opacity

Import Illustrator file as layers

  • File > Import > File, select your Illustrator document and import it as Composition – Retain Layer Sizes
  • You will not be able to adjust/animate the shapes you import from Illustrator in the same way that you can those you created in After Effects — they will be like any other imported image. (See this link for how to convert imported vector graphics to shape layer)

Text

  • Use horizontal Type Tool at top of screen to create text box
  • Text is fairly straightforward / typical of other Adobe programs
    • Character and Paragraph windows
    • Set size, typeface, color, adjust kerning, alignment, more
  • To outline text, Layer > Create Shapes from Text

Pre-composing

  • Makes a single layer out of several layers
  • Select all layers you wish to combine then:
    • Layer > Pre-compose or Control + click and select Pre-compose
  • If you wish to edit the elements individually again, just double click on the pre-comp layer in the timeline

Audio

  • Drag and drop on the timeline like all other other footage
  • Audio levels influences volume
    • Can add keyframes to this to adjust volume over time
  • Waveform shows visual representation of audio
  • Layer markers — Layer > Add Marker or Control+8
    • Layer markers are not keyframes, do not influence anything, just a reference tool

Rendering

  • Choose the composition you wish to render
  • Select Composition > Add to Render Queue
  • Determine export settings:
    • Render Settings: generally leave default Best Settings
    • Output module:
      • For high quality .mov files, leave default Lossless Settings (Quality: Best, Resolution: Full)
      • For best Vimeo uploads, open Output Module window, click Format Options Button, select H.264 from Video Codec dropdown
      • Make sure your audio is on
    • Output To: determine file name and where you will save the file
  • Click Render button when you’re ready
  • Notes
    • The files in your After Effects project are linked, just like in an InDesign document. If planning on working on both a personal computer and the computers in the lab, make sure to keep a centrally located, well-organized folder of files used in your motion graphic since you will have to transfer this folder along with your After Effects file if you plan to work on different computers.
    • Re: Adobe Creative Cloud and Adobe Creative Suite. If planning to work on a laptop/computer outside of the lab with After Effects CS5/CS5, you will not be able to open Creative Cloud files in the older versions of After Effects—while it’s fairly problem-free to open CS5/CS6 files in Creative Cloud. Keep this in mind as you’re working. (See this link for saving to previous version of After Effects)
    • The computers in the lab likely have more RAM than a laptop, and will be faster at previewing and rendering.
